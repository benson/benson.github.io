chrome variations · mtgcollection

two contrasting layout directions for the collection viewer chrome. variation a leans into structure with a persistent border frame around the cards; variation b strips chrome to the bare minimum and lets the cards fill the canvas, with overlay tools on demand. pick one or a hybrid.

a · border frame

header + left filters + right add/stats + footer wrap the grid. everything is one click away, nothing is hidden.

b · content-first canvas

slim top strip, cards span the full width, floating action buttons reveal tools and overlays on demand. click the + to see the modal pattern.

a · trade-offs

fastest to scan and act on: filters, search, stats, and add-cards are always visible without a click. cost is real estate — the card grid lives inside ~660px of width on a 1280px viewport, so cards feel small and dense. best for power users who treat the app like a workbench.

b · trade-offs

the cards become the product. nothing competes for attention except the search bar, and the page reads almost like a gallery. cost is discoverability — filters and stats live behind fab clicks, and someone new to the app may not realize they exist. best when the collection itself is the story.

pick a, b, or a hybrid — feedback welcome
