returning player briefing

welcome back: what changed since 2024

you last played around the january 2024 early-access launch. the game that exists now is substantially bigger and much smoother in the first ten hours. palworld 1.0 shipped july 10, 2026 — free update, still $29.99, with full crossplay (steam / xbox / ps5 / mac) and day-one game pass.

what actually matters for your first ten hours

  • catching is way faster changed — capture rates were buffed globally, and the capture bonus now completes at 5 catches per species instead of 10–12. the sphere-throw ui shows your live counter per species.
  • dodge is now a dash 1.0 — and you can attack and reload mid-dash. your old "roll = commitment" muscle memory is obsolete. there's also auto-run.
  • normal difficulty no longer drops your items on death changed — the single biggest early-game frustration is gone by default.
  • missions matter now 1.0 — the story/mission system was completely rebuilt into a guided arc that ends at the world tree. missions pay big xp lumps and gate progression. do them; don't skip out of 2024 habit.
  • tower bosses have 5-minute timers changed (was 10) with reduced hp — they're dps checks now, and the arenas are region-themed with mid-fight tactic shifts.
  • work suitability goes to 10 levels (was 4), condensation needs 48 copies (was 116), low-level pals gain xp faster, and egg incubation is halved in new worlds.
  • raids got friendlier — wave-based with warnings, they scale to your worker count, and you can pay a negotiator to cancel one.
old save?

your ea save still works in 1.0 — levels, pals, and items carry over — but mission progress resets to fit the new story structure. for a 2-year gap, honestly: start fresh. the early game is the best part and it's been rebalanced end to end.

setup

before you start: world settings

at world creation you pick casual / normal / hard, or customize. nothing is locked in — you can change difficulty and every slider later from the main menu, so don't agonize. hard is tuned for dedicated servers; casual removes most friction if you just want to vibe.

recommended: start from normal, then tweak these:

settingset towhy
death penaltyno drops1.0 normal already keeps your equipment; "no drops" also keeps inventory. losing 40 minutes of ore to a fall is not fun difficulty.
egg incubation time0 (instant)the single best qol tweak. wild eggs become a free-pal pipeline instead of a wall-clock timer. no downside while learning.
supply drop interval15–60 mindefault is 3 hours; drops are fun free loot.
gather/drop rates1x (or 1.5x)1x is well-tuned in 1.0. bump slightly if grinding annoys you.
xp rateleave at 1xcapture-bonus xp ignores this multiplier anyway, and overleveling trivializes the fun bosses.

character creation is purely cosmetic (no stats), and you can change your appearance later at an antique dresser. there's also hardcore mode (permadeath for you and your pals) and random pal spawn mode — fun, but not for a return-from-2-years playthrough.

orientation

how the game works (the loop)

palworld is a survival-crafting game where the automation layer is monster collecting. everything feeds one loop:

  1. catch pals with pal spheres — this is also your main xp source, not combat.
  2. assign pals to your base, where each works jobs matching its work suitability — logging, mining, planting, cooking, transporting. your base becomes a factory that runs while you're out.
  3. spend materials in the tech tree using technology points to unlock better gear, spheres, and base buildings.
  4. fight or catch bigger things — alpha pals, dungeons, and eventually tower bosses — which unlock ancient technology points for special unlocks.

the golden rule that the game undersells: throw spheres at everything, including bosses. catching gives dramatically more xp than killing, and the first 5 catches of each species pay an escalating bonus. a "catch everything that moves" playstyle out-levels a "kill everything" playstyle by a wide margin.

dotted-underlined terms throughout this guide have hover/tap definitions, and there's a full glossary at the bottom.

hours 0–1 · levels 1–5

survival, first catches, first camp

you wake up on the plateau of beginnings in windswept hills, around (228, -477). this whole region is mild-climate (no temperature damage), stocked with level 1–15 pals, and has the highest dungeon density on the map. it's a tutorial zone the size of a small country.

tip

base missions listed at the palbox ("build a campfire", "deploy a pal"...) level your base up — but structures only count if they're built inside the palbox radius. also: build on flat ground. pals pathfind like roombas, and slopes break their tiny brains.

core mechanic deep-dive

catching 101

this is the most important system in the game, so here's everything:

why catching beats killing

  • the capture bonus is the single largest xp source in the early game. it scales with your level (not the target's), so catching a level 2 lamball at level 15 still pays well.
  • in 1.0 the bonus escalates over the first 5 catches of each species, then stops. the sphere-throw ui shows the live remaining count — trust that counter. loop: park in a dense spawn area, chain-catch to 5, rotate to the next species.
  • this applies to alphas and dungeon bosses too. always catch bosses, never just kill them. a boss catch is worth a huge chunk of a level plus ancient tech points.

mechanics that raise your odds

  • weaken first. low hp = higher catch chance. status effects (burn, shock, etc.) help further.
  • back bonus: spheres that hit a pal's back get bonus capture power. crouch-approach unaware pals, or kite around behind them after engaging.
  • lifmunk effigies: small green-glowing statues scattered everywhere. turn them in at a statue of power (buildable at base, or use one at an abandoned church) for permanent, account-wide capture power. there are 14 capture-power levels and only 51 effigies are needed to max it — grab every single one you see, and the early ranks are cheap. (the infamous 2024 bug where effigies lowered your catch rate was fixed long ago.)
  • better spheres: plain pal spheres fall off hard by level ~13. mega spheres unlock at level 14 — craft them the moment you can, and keep 30+ spheres on you at all times.
  • humans are catchable. low odds, but catching syndicate thugs is legal (no wanted level), and they can do light handiwork at base or be sold. do not attack civilians — that summons the pidf, who are level 40+ and will delete you.

specialists > generalists

a pal with one work skill (foxparks: kindling only) will always be at its station. a 5-skill generalist wanders off to do whatever it noticed last. assign single-skill specialists to fixed jobs, and note that higher suitability level = faster work — a kindling-2 pal smelts visibly faster than kindling-1. in 1.0, suitability runs to level 10.

economy

tech tree spending guide (levels 1–15)

you earn 1–2 technology points per level, plus bonuses from great eagle statues and dungeons. they're tight until ~level 20, so spend deliberately.

  • decorative anything, extra outfit recolors, torches past the first couple

ancient technology points

ancient tech points come from first-time boss catches (alphas, dungeon bosses) and tower-boss first clears (the first tower pays 5). spend order:

  1. egg incubator (level 7, 1 point) — with instant incubation set, wild eggs are the strongest free-pal pipeline in the game.
  2. grappling gun (level 12) — movement tech that trivializes cliffs and weight problems.
  3. hot-spring upgrades and the rest — later.
hours 3–5 · levels 9–12

mounts, dungeons, and your first alpha

egg route

wild eggs sit on cliff edges and ledges everywhere. with the incubator + instant incubation, an egg-collecting detour on every trip quietly fills your party with pals you haven't even fought yet — including species above your level.

management

keeping your base alive

base leveling

complete the base missions listed at the palbox → claim base upgrades. each base level ≈ +1 worker slot (defaults: 15 workers per base, cap 30 levels). base level 10 unlocks a second base, level 15 a third — and base level is account-wide, so new bases start at full capacity. the classic second base is a dedicated mining outpost placed directly on ore nodes; good candidates: (71, -408) with 8–9 nodes, or (7, -527) with 8 nodes and big flat ground.

the SAN spiral (and how to never see it)

every pal has SAN (sanity, 0–100). overwork, hunger, and sleeping on the ground drain it; low SAN pals slack, get injured, and eventually break down with an ulcer. prevention is cheap:

  • one bed per pal, no exceptions. straw beds are fine.
  • feed box always stocked with cooked food, near the workstations (long walks to eat = long breaks from work).
  • hot spring (level 12) — pals soak and restore SAN passively. build two.
  • don't leave a 500-item craft queued around the clock — that's the overwork engine.

1.0 raid notes

raids now arrive in waves with a warning, scale to your worker count, and defense structures no longer consume ammo. early on you can ignore them entirely (your work pals will brawl it out) or pay the negotiator to cancel one. if raids annoy you on principle, the plateau at (191, -39) near the sealed realm of the guardian teleport is famously unreachable by ground raids — a good spot for base #2 or a later relocation.

weapons (first 10 hours)

armor & environment

  • cloth outfit (6) → pelt armor (11) → metal much later (33).
  • always wear a shield — it's a regenerating hp barrier, and the first tier is nearly free.
  • tundra outfit (tier ~9) handles night chill and the snow biome; heat-resistant pelt armor (tier 16) handles the desert. rule of thumb: cold-resist for night/snow, heat-resist for desert, and merchants sell both if you'd rather not craft.

1.0 combat feel

  • dash, don't roll. dodge is a dash now and you can shoot and reload mid-dash. fights are much more mobile than you remember.
  • your pal is half your dps. party pals were buffed repeatedly — bring an attacker, use the new "attack that enemy!" targeting command (mouse wheel / right stick) to focus fire.
  • elements matter: fire→grass/ice, water→fire, ground→electric, dark→neutral, dragon→dark. that ground→electric line is about to be relevant.

hours 8–10 · levels 15–20

wings, effigy runs, and the first tower

zoe & grizzbolt

rayne syndicate tower · northwest of spawn, windswept hills · ~(112, -434)
boss level 10
element electric
weak to ground
timer 5:00 (1.0, was 10)
first clear 5 ancient tech points

the fight is a dps check now — 1.0 halved the timer and cut the hp to match, so defensive stalling fails. doable at 12–15 with ground pals, comfortable at 18+. send your pal in to tank and hold aggro, keep shooting, and use the pillars to break line of sight on the minigun sweep and the crosshair-targeted beam. dash (with mid-dash reloads) through everything else. if you wipe, you're ejected to the entrance — walk back in and try again.

beating it unlocks the tower's fast travel and marks the real start of the game: eight more towers wait at levels 20 / 30 / 40 / 50 / 55 / 60 / 68 / 80, each with a region behind it. (post-fight quests in 1.0 even let you eventually recruit defeated tower-boss pals.)

that's the ten-hour mark. if you're around level 18–20 with a flying mount, a self-feeding base, a ranch economy, and the first tower down — you're exactly on curve.

numbers

stat points & leveling meta

where your stat points go

you get 1 point per level. 1.0 cut base carry weight from 500 to 300, which settles the debate:

statper pointverdict
weight+50pump first, to ~600–800. encumbrance is the real end boss.
stamina+10second priority, to ~200 — climbing, dashing, gliding all eat it.
hp+100third, toward ~1000–1500 as tower fights get real.
attack+2only if you personally shoot a lot; your pals scale harder.
work speed+50zero points. base pals do the crafting, not you.
no respec for a while

respec exists (memory wiping medicine) but it's gated behind a level ~43 workbench — effectively not available in your first ten hours. don't dump points into attack/work speed "to try it".

xp sources, ranked

  1. capture bonus chains (scales with your level; ignores the xp world-setting multiplier)
  2. missions — main and sub 1.0 (big lumps; also gate progression)
  3. alpha / dungeon boss captures
  4. dungeons and bounty targets
  5. base crafting trickle, fast-travel unlock bonuses

party pals level with you (capped at your level); box pals at base don't gain combat xp but keep working. rotate future combat pals into your party early so they keep pace.

anti-patterns

14 mistakes to avoid

  1. killing instead of catching. spheres at everything, bosses included. it's the xp game.
  2. living on raw berries. cook everything; starving drains hp. the feed bag tech auto-feeds you later.
  3. ignoring weight. points into weight, cattivas in party, and dump ore into chests before you're crawling.
  4. no beds / no hot spring. the SAN spiral starts silently and ends with your whole base in the infirmary.
  5. building on slopes or outside the palbox ring. pathing breaks; mission builds outside the ring don't count.
  6. no transporting pal. harvested food rots on the ground instead of reaching the feed box.
  7. generalist pals on critical stations. single-skill specialists (foxparks on kindling) never wander off.
  8. hoarding materials "for later". check the tech tree every level; dismantling refunds materials, so building mistakes are cheap. tech points are the scarce thing, not stone.
  9. condensing early. even at 1.0's 48 copies, condensation is a mid-game project. early dupes → 5-catch bonus, then sell.
  10. only leveling workers. party pals cap at your level and box pals don't gain xp — keep your future fighters in the party.
  11. attacking civilians. wanted level → level 40+ pidf response → death. catch syndicate thugs instead; that's legal.
  12. skipping missions out of 2024 habit. they're a top-two xp source now and gate fast travel.
  13. wandering into 1.0 endgame zones. the world tree is level-80 content, and wildlife sanctuaries have drone patrols plus trespassing wanted status.
  14. fighting like it's 2024. dash-cancel, mid-dash reloads, focus-fire commands. the combat is faster than you remember — use it.
the road ahead

after hour ten

the shape of the next fifty hours, so you know what you're building toward:

  • towers 2–5 — lily & lyleen (20), axel & orserk (30), marcus & faleris (40), victor & shadowbeak (50) — each gates a new region of the original map.
  • breeding (~19) — the breeding farm + cake economy; 1.0's mutation system gives every hatch a small chance at boosted stats and unique passives. the pal surgery table (from the terraria update) lets you overwrite passives directly, which massively de-rngs the old grind.
  • metal & electricity era (~30s) — mining base on ore/coal, refined ingots, generators, sphere factories.
  • fishing — added in 2025, a whole side economy with salvage.
  • sakurajima → feybreak → sunreach → the world tree — the post-2024 continents, in roughly that level order, ending at an actual story conclusion at level 80. there's a real ending now. wild.
