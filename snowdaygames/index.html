snow day games

couch-friendly psychological horror picks for ellie & benson.
no fast camera movement. just vibes, dread, and blankets.

little nightmares

puzzle platformer 2.5d side-scrolling ~4 hours

you play as a tiny kid in a yellow raincoat, sneaking through a massive, grotesque ship full of oversized creatures trying to catch you. it plays like a dark animated short film — you're hiding under tables, squeezing through vents, and solving little environmental puzzles to escape each room. the whole thing is creepy and beautiful at the same time. no combat, just tension and atmosphere.

camera: fixed cinematic angle, very smooth and steady. perfect couch game — easy to follow along.

darkwood

survival horror top-down ~15-20 hours

a top-down survival horror game set in a nightmarish, overgrown forest somewhere in eastern europe. during the day you explore the woods, scavenge materials, and talk to unsettling characters. at night you barricade yourself inside a hideout and try to survive until morning — things bang on the walls, lights flicker, and you never quite see what's out there. the atmosphere is relentless. no jump scares, just a slow, building sense that everything in this forest is deeply wrong.

camera: top-down bird's eye view, very steady. great to watch — the limited visibility makes it tense even from the couch.

slay the princess — the pristine cut

visual novel hand-drawn ~8-10 hours

a choose-your-own-adventure psychological horror game with gorgeous hand-drawn black and white art. a narrator tells you to go into a cabin and slay a princess. that's it. but nothing is what it seems — your choices reshape who the princess is, who you are, and what the story even means. fully voice acted with incredible performances. it's creepy, funny, philosophical, and surprisingly emotional. basically an interactive horror graphic novel.

camera: completely static — still images and text. zero motion sickness risk. the best one to play together since you can debate every choice.
